🐛 Bugs I Found — as a Real User

Bug 1 — Offline downloads that aren't actually offline

I specifically downloaded lectures so I could study during my commute without burning data. Later, with internet off, I opened the app and hit a wall — content wouldn't play. The app still needed a live connection for content I'd already downloaded.

For a student who planned their entire study session around that download — on a train, in a hostel with patchy WiFi — that's a broken promise. And for a learning app competing in the GATE prep space where trust is everything, broken promises hurt more than bugs do.

Bug 2 — Video playback error on minimize and return

Mid-lecture, I got a notification, minimized the app, came back. Playback error. Black screen. Had to reload and scroll back to find my spot.

When you're deep inside understanding a concept, that interruption doesn't just waste 30 seconds — it breaks your mental thread entirely. Some students will just close the app at that point. That's a retention problem disguised as a minor bug.

💡 What I Think Is Genuinely Missing

GoClasses has solid content — that's the hard part, you've done that. But the app doesn't yet give students a reason to come back every single day beyond willpower alone. And willpower runs out. Systems don't.

🏆 Gamification with real rewards

Every completed lecture earns points. Hit a weekly threshold and unlock a free test series for that subject. Now students aren't just watching content — they're working toward something tangible. Completion rates go up, watch time goes up, retention goes up. It's not gimmicky — it's aligned with exactly what GATE students care about: measurable progress.

📊 Leaderboard

GATE is a rank-based exam. Students preparing for it are already wired for competition — they compare scores, check rank predictors. A leaderboard showing top learners by subject or weekly activity is not a distraction — it's fuel. Students will push each other without you doing anything.

🔥 Daily activity heatmap and watch-time stats

GATE aspirants are obsessed with tracking effort. Hours studied, days streak, subjects covered — they journal this manually right now. If GoClasses gave them that mirror inside the app, they'd open it every morning just to see their own progress. That's a habit loop you'd be building for free.
